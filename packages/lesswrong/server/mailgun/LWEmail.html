I've been running LessWrong for 8 years now! Lots of stuff has happened in that period, most of which I won't go into in this email (my fundraiser post from last year covers that).

In very short summary:

LessWrong 1.0 was founded by Eliezer Yudkowsky in 2010. It died in 2016 from spammers, outdated software, and a lack of moderation. I founded a team to revive it; we succeeded, and now it's more active than ever. After COVID, we expanded operations with funding from Open Philanthropy, the Survival and Flourishing Fund, and the ill-fated FTX Foundation. We built Lighthaven, a successful conference and research center in Berkeley making $3M/yr in revenue. However, in 2022, FTX's ill-fate came to pass. In 2024, FTX sued us to claw back their donations, and around the same time Open Philanthropy's biggest donor asked them to exit our funding area. We almost went bankrupt.

But we ran a big fundraiser, cut our salaries for a while, I donated a lot of my own money, and we pulled through!

Now, what did we do with the time we bought in 2025? At a high level, we grew LessWrong faster than basically any previous year (while I think also improving on content and discussion quality), we filled Lighthaven with lots of events and got very close to financially breaking even on that part of our operations, and worked on a bunch of big communications projects including AI 2027.
